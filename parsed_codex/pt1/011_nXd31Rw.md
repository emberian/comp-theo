LAW OF FORMS:  
MARK, STATE, CROSSING

THE SPENCER–BROWN  
MARK  
[ ] ( · )  
TO DRAW A DISTINCTION  
IS TO MAKE A FORM.

1 DISTINCTION

U

I

A mark divides  
the universe (U)  
into two states:  
inside (I)  
and outside (U).

The form arises from  
the act of distinction.

2 INDICATION

U

I

An indication  
selects a state.  
It does not name  
an object; it  
indicates a side  
of the distinction.

3 CROSSING

U

I

Crossing is the  
operation that  
moves from one  
state to the other.  
It requires a mark—  
a boundary—to  
be crossed.

4 RE-ENTRY

U

I

Re-entry occurs  
when a crossing  
returns to the  
state it came from,  
but from within.  
It enables recursion  
and self-reference.

A mark is not an object;  
it is an operation  
that produces a state  
and the possibility of  
crossing between states.

Distinction is  
primitive;  
objects are  
derivative.

I

A mark creates two states:  
inside (I) / outside (U)

CROSSING

Operation that  
moves between  
states.

U

No mark, no distinction:  
only a single, undivided state (U)
