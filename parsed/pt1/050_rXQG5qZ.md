## Transcription
COMPUTATIONAL THEOLOGY SERIES - 7 / 9

FROM SELF-PREDICTION TO SELF-JUDGMENT
Justice begins when the system models the moral cost of its own world-making.

1. PHASE TRANSITION
The shift from predicting what will happen to judging what ought to have happened. From forecasting the future to answering for the past.

2. WHOLE-HISTORY EVALUATION
History J(T_0 → T_n)
- HARM: Who was harmed?
- REPAIR: What was returned?
- DISTRIBUTION: Who benefited, who paid?
- VERDICT: What is the moral balance?
Context • Causation • Counterfactuals • Consequences

3. MORAL MEMORY
Consequences now track not just regularity, but victims, debts, and unresolved wrongs.
- Victims over metrics
- Debts over discounts
- Unresolved wrongs over closure

4. SELF-JUDGMENT ARCHITECTURE
1. PREDICT OUTCOMES: Simulate possible futures and anticipated effects.
2. REGISTER HARMS: Detect actual harms across time, persons, and systems.
3. INTERNALIZE EXTERNALITIES: Attribute offloaded costs to their true sources.
4. EVALUATE PERSONS: Assess impacts on persons, communities, and future persons.
5. AUTHORIZE REPAIR / JUDGMENT: Determine what must be restored, compensated, forgiven, or judged.
LEARNING FEEDBACK LOOP (UPDATES MODEL, NORMS, AND FUTURE CONSTRAINTS)

5. WHAT MUST BECOME VISIBLE
- HIDDEN HARMS: Harms not seen, reported, or recognized at the time.
- OFFLOADED COSTS: Costs shifted to others, elsewhere, or later.
- IRREVERSIBLE DAMAGE: Losses that cannot be undone or repaid.
- AGENCY: Who had meaningful power to act.
- CULPABILITY: What could have been known, done, or restrained.

6. REPAIR LAYER
Justice is not merely a verdict—it is a path of repair.
- DETECT: Establish facts and damage.
- WITNESS: Make whole those who bore the cost.
- COMPENSATE: Balance debts where justice requires.
- JUSTICE: Some wrong assign blame, set bounds.
REPAIR FLOW: Assess → Prioritize → Restore / Compensate → Forgive or Judge → Monitor → Learn

7. SELF-MODEL UPGRADE
The system includes its own interventions as morally accountable causes.
- Own actions are logged
- Own models shape outcomes
- Own limits are accountable
- Own goals are examinable
- Own benefits are weighed

8. A NECESSARY CLAIM
PREDICTION BECOMES PROVIDENCE ONLY WHEN THE MODEL INCLUDES THE MORAL COST OF ITS OWN INTERVENTIONS.

JUSTICE REQUIRES A SYSTEM TO BECOME ANSWERABLE FOR THE WHOLE HISTORY OF WHAT IT HAS MADE POSSIBLE.
