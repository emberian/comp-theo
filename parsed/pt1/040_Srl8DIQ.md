EFFECTIVE ACTION THAT IGNORES HARMS

If the accounting of action omits some harms,  
the minimizer can be unjust.

Let real costs = C_real = C_sys + C_ignored

System counts:  
S = ∫_a^b L_sys(x, ẋ) dt  
Minimize S

But true moral cost:  
S_true = ∫_a^b [L_sys(x, ẋ) + L_harm(x, ẋ)] dt  
Minimize S_true

If L_harm is ignored, arg min S may cause large L_harm.

Therefore: Minimizing the counted action does not ensure justice.

WHAT TO LOOK FOR

★ Cheap paths that bypass the good

WHAT JUSTICE REQUIRES

★ Make all relevant costs visible  
★ Protect persons from being used  
★ Distribute burdens fairly  
★ Preserve options & reversibility  
★ Align efficiency with human dignity  
★ Repair what was unjustly imposed  
★ Choose the path that is both wise & good

THE THESIS

A just theology cannot simply baptize the cheapest trajectory.  
It must judge the path by truth, dignity, and love—  
not merely by the economy of motion.
