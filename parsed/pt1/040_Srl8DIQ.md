## Transcription
ORDERED CREATION / JUSTICE & LEAST-ACTION SERIES - 6 / 9

WHERE THE COMPOSITION FAILS
How low action can coexist with suffering, externalized cost, and injustice.

THE DIAGNOSTIC MAP
Least action (or low action) is not a moral guarantee. Their failure modes explain how systems harming, or indifferent outcomes can still be dynamically cheap.

Look for:
- Unstable results
- Bypass of the good
- Dispossession & damping
- Historical lock-in
- Scale mismatch
- Indifference to persons

FAILURE MODES
1. LOCAL MINIMA VS GLOBAL GOOD: The system settles in an easy basin that blocks access to better states.
2. HIDDEN INTERACTION COSTS: Costs are pushed into weakly coupled parts of the system and stay unseen.
3. EXTERNALIZED SUFFERING: Harm is displaced onto reservoirs of persons or ecosystems.
4. PATH DEPENDENCE / HYSTERESIS: Early choices constrain the future; returning to the good becomes costly.
5. NON-ADDITIVE ACROSS SCALES: Cheap actions at one scale create expensive effects at another.
6. INDIFFERENT SELECTION ACROSS PERSONS: Persons are treated as interchangeable tokens; who bears the cost is ignored.

MORAL WARNING
What is cheap for the system may be costly for persons.

EFFICIENCY IS JUSTICE
Two ways to measure the same world.
SYSTEM-LEVEL EFFICIENCY:
- Minimizes total action
- Aggregates over parts
- Ignores distribution
- Silent on persons
- Can stabilize harm
PERSON-LEVEL JUSTICE:
- Protects persons
- Counts who is burdened
- Requires rights & dignity
- Seeks fair opportunity
- Demands repair

STATE-SPACE DIAGRAM: CHEAP PATHS THAT FAIL
Cheap path (low action) — Morally valuable path — Good damping / suffering flow — Attractor
Starting condition — Moral valley (harder path) — Cheap Attractor (dynamically stable) — Reservoir of suffering (people, places, ecologies)
The cheap path avoids morally valuable states and its valleys cost data a reservoir of suffering.

LEARNING WITHOUT VALUE
(From the foundational Genesis Lens)
Learning optimizes what is usable - information that increases stability, predictability, or control.
But it can remain blind to value.
This system learns what works, not what is right.

EFFECTIVE ACTION THAT IGNORES HARM
If the accounting of action omits cost terms, the minimizer can be unjust:
S_eff = ∫ L_sys dt    L_sys = T - V_sys
S_full = ∫ (L_sys + L_harm) dt
If S_eff is ignored, any state-S may cause state-harm.
Therefore: minimizing the counted action does not track justice.

WHY UNCARING NATURE MATTERS
Because blind (works) selection can stabilize structures that are dynamically efficient yet morally unacceptable.
Stably without — Careful — Stable adaptor — Stable harm

- Stability without conscience is not justice
- Nature does not aim at the good
- Only persons can recognize & respond to it

WHAT TO LOOK FOR
- Cheap paths that bypass the good
- Hidden & displaced costs
- People or places used as sinks
- Lock-in that preserves unjust states
- Mismatch at one scale, harm at another
- Indifference to who suffers
- No mechanism for repair or restitution

DIAGNOSTIC QUESTIONS
- Who (or what) pays the real cost?
- What options were foreclosed early?
- What is made invisible by aggregation?
- Is the path reversible without great harm?
- Does the scale-up behave unjustly?
- Are persons treated as ends or means?

WHAT JUSTICE REQUIRES
- Make all relevant costs visible
- Protect persons from being used
- Distribute burdens fairly
- Preserve options at reversibility
- Align efficiency with human dignity
- Repair what was already required
- Choose the path that is both costs & good

THE THESIS
A just theology cannot simply baptize the cheapest trajectory.
It must judge the path by truth, dignity, and love -
not merely by the economy of motion.
