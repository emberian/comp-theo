EFFECTIVE ACTION
S_eff[p] = S_core[p] + S_boundary[p] + S_diss[p]
S_core[p] = action accumulated within the ordered core
S_boundary[p] = cost/benefit of crossing or using boundaries
S_diss[p] = dissipation from export of disorder or energy
Open boundaries can make some trajectories effectively cheaper or more stable by absorbing unwanted return flow.

WHY OPENNESS MATTERS
1 EXPORT OF DISORDER: Offload entropy, noise, and errors to reservoirs.
2 PREVENTION OF BACK-REFLECTION: Reduces returns, echoes, and cyclic waste.
3 STABILIZATION OF SELECTED CHANNELS: Favors useful flows and useful information.
4 MAINTENANCE OF LOCAL ORDER: Keeps the core coherent, responsive, and adaptive.

TRAJECTORY LANDSCAPE
CLOSED SYSTEM (no tails)
return (well up rim)
back-reflection traps trajectories
Confinement raises effective action. Trajectories face more returns and less escape.

OPEN SYSTEM (with tails/reservoirs)
dissipation lowers return cost
escape channels reduce effective cost
Outflow lowers effective action. Trajectories can leave, dissipate, or be filtered.

LIMIT TO SERIES: INFINITE TAILS & PROTECTED SUBSPACES
- Infinite tails (semi-infinite) provide the space and capacity for dissipation.
- Protected subspaces in the core orbit which patterns are worth keeping.

INTERPRETATION
Least action in an open world is not purely internal; the environment enters the bookkeeping.
internal order → boundary exchange → dissipation = effective action
What leaves, what stays, and how it is routed — all affect S_eff.

THESIS PARAPHRASE
A worldsystem may minimize action not in isolation, but by trading with an exterior that absorbs, stabilizes, or filters trajectories.
