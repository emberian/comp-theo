WHY OPENNESS MATTERS

1 EXPORT OF DISORDER  
Offloads entropy, noise, and errors to reservoirs.

2 PREVENTION OF BACK-REFLECTION  
Reduces returns, echoes, and cyclic waste.

3 STABILIZATION OF SELECTED CHANNELS  
Favors useful flows and useful information.

4 MAINTENANCE OF LOCAL ORDER  
Keeps the core coherent, responsive, and adaptive.

TRAJECTORY LANDSCAPE

CLOSED SYSTEM (no tails)

returns build up cost

traps trajectories

OPEN SYSTEM (with tails/reservoirs)

dissipation lowers  
return cost

cost

Outflow lowers effective action.  
Trajectories can leave, dissipate, or be filtered.

## Visual

The poster uses a structured infographic layout with stacked panels, heavy black condensed headings, red emphasis, teal section labels, and a cream paper texture. The central schematic shows an ordered core connected to reservoir/tail regions, with black, red, and dashed trajectories illustrating retained paths, exiting paths, and dissipative flow. Lower panels compare closed versus open action landscapes using 3D contour-like terrain diagrams, then connect the idea to earlier “infinite tails” and “protected subspaces” concepts through small symbolic diagrams.
