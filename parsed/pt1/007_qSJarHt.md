1 Ordinary case
Real symmetric adjacency constrains the dynamics. Many transport and mixing phenomena obey no-go theorems in this setting.

2 Chiral construction
Replace ordinary edge weights by a unitary signing σ. The walk uses a Hermitian matrix A^σ whose edges carry phases.

3 Formal condition
σ_uv is a unit complex number and σ_vu = σ̄_uv. Orientation changes interference without sacrificing Hermiticity.

4 Interpretive point
The same underlying graph can acquire new capacities once possibility is oriented rather than merely connected.

phase on edges    e^{iφ}    unitary signing

Consequences
Chirality can enable state transfer or uniform mixing that are forbidden, or much harder, in the ordinary case.
