1  
Metaphysical reading

The firmament is not a thing among things.  
It is the condition under which above and  
below, inside and outside, can be  
meaningfully distinct.

2  
Graph-theoretic reading

Let G = (V,E). Vertices represent states;  
edges represent admissible transitions.  
A boundary, partition, or quotient  
organizes which relations are possible  
and how strongly.

Formal sketch

Graph = (V,E)  
node = state  
edge = possible transition  
boundary = firmament  
region = realm

3  
Why distinction matters

Creation begins not by filling an  
undifferentiated whole with objects,  
but by instituting separability,  
adjacency, and lawful passage.

Key claim

To create first is to differentiate;  
to differentiate is not to destroy  
relation, but to render relation  
intelligible.
