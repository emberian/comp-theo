AN ACTION-MINIMIZING ORDERED CREATION

Final synthesis: when recursive least action does and does not work.

"A world's structure is the shadow cast by its cheapest ways of becoming."

SYNTHESIS ARCHITECTURE

2 poset (order): Relations constrain possibilities; partial order emerges.
3 cover graph: Cover relations identify what can change locally.
4 local action rule: L(x_t, x_t) — Costs are assigned to local moves (least action rule).
5 path ensemble: Trajectories compete; low-action paths dominate.

7 emergent goods: Stable, intelligible, often valuable structure appears.

RECURSION WORKS WHEN...
- composable local actions: Local costs can be composed without hidden terms.
- stable interfaces: Boundaries transmit predictable, low-cost summaries.
- scale separation: Relevant scales weakly interact; coarse-graining holds.
- protected subspaces: Robust sets persist; small perturbations stay contained.
- compatible objectives: Local objectives align with higher-level aims.
- filtered openness: Novelty enters through low-cost, informative channels.

RECURSION FAILS WHEN...
- hidden interaction costs: Unmodeled couplings make composition non-additive.
- local/global conflict: Locally optimal moves conflict at larger scales.
- excessive feedback: Tight loops amplify small errors and costs.
- frustrated orientation: Incompatible constraints prevent coherent ordering.
- unstable boundaries: Interfaces drift; coherence cannot be maintained.

EQUATIONS SUMMARY
S[p] = Σ_t L(x_t, x_{t+1})  (path action)
p* = argmin_p S[p]  (least action)
S_total = Σ_s S_s  under weak interaction / good coarse-graining (composability condition)

RECURSIVE WORLDSYSTEMS
SUCCESS: recursion least action
Local economies compose cleanly. Interfaces are stable; costs add; structure recurs across scales.
FAILURE: recursion breaks
Hidden couplings and conflict break composability. Action escalates with scale.

COMPOSABILITY CONDITION
For a partition of the system into subsystems s with interfaces ∂s:
S_total ≈ Σ_s S_s + S_int
Recursion works when:
S_int ≪ Σ_s S_s
(Interfaces are cheap; information loss is small.)

METAPHYSICAL READING
If reality were organized by lawful minimization of action, structured goods might emerge as indirect consequences of recursively compatible local economies. Order, function, and value would not be imposed from above, but licensed by the way possibilities can least costfully cohere.
"Good is not the goal; it is what remains when low-cost order is given room to become."

Minimizing action is not the same as maximizing good.
Yet under the right order-theoretic and dynamical conditions, a world engineered for low action may recursively produce stable, intelligible, and even desirable forms.
