## Transcription
COMPUTATIONAL THEOLOGY SERIES - 1 / 9

CAN A JUST GOD EMERGE FROM AN UNSUPERVISED SELF-PREDICTING SYSTEM?
Autodidaxis, blowtorch formation, and the gap between prediction and justice.

1. THE CORE QUESTION
A self-predicting universe may learn laws, stabilize regularities, and shape its own future data. But can such a system become just, or does justice require a higher evaluative layer?

2. THREE INGREDIENTS
- Autodidactic nature: Unsupervised learning of effective law.
- Blowtorch formation: Directed energetic carving of structure.
- Justice: Evaluation of histories by more than efficiency.

3. WORKING THESIS
PREDICTION GIVES LAW.
BLOWTORCH DYNAMICS GIVES STRUCTURE.
JUSTICE APPEARS ONLY WHEN SELF-PREDICTION BECOMES ANSWERABLE SELF-JUDGMENT.

4. FROM SELF-PREDICTION TO SELF-JUDGMENT
1. PREDICT WORLD: p(x_{t+1} | x_t, θ)
2. ACT ON WORLD: a_t → world
3. RESHAPE FUTURE DATA: p_future = f(a_t)
4. LEARN UPDATED MODEL: θ_{t+1} = learn(D_{t+1})
5. EVALUATE WHOLE HISTORY: J(history)
THE FINAL STEP DOES NOT FOLLOW AUTOMATICALLY

5. WHAT UNSUPERVISED SYSTEMS CAN DO
- improve predictive fit
- stabilize effective laws
- compress information
- discover attractors
- self-sample and update

6. WHAT THEY DO NOT AUTOMATICALLY DO
- protect persons
- count hidden harms
- repair victims
- distinguish efficiency from righteousness
- justify their own formative violence
EFFICIENCY IS NOT JUSTICE

7. THE GAP
PREDICTION
STRUCTURE
JUSTICE
A system may master the first two while failing at the third.

8. SERIES ROADMAP
1. Frame question  2. Autodidactic component  3. Blowtorch formation  4. Composition landscape  5. Trouble Step  6. Tropical justice  7. Verdict integrate

9. FINAL HOOK
A UNIVERSE CAN LEARN TO PREDICT ITSELF WITHOUT LEARNING TO FORGIVE ITSELF.

THIS SERIES ASKS WHETHER DIVINE JUSTICE CAN EMERGE FROM LAW-LEARNING ALONE, OR WHETHER GOODNESS MUST ENTER AS A HIGHER-ORDER CONSTRAINT.
