## Transcription
LAW OF FORMS: MARK, STATE, CROSSING

THE SPENCER-BROWN MARK
[ ] ( · )

TO DRAW A DISTINCTION IS TO MAKE A FORM.

1 DISTINCTION
A mark divides the universe (U) into two states: inside (I) and outside (U). The form arises from the act of distinction.

2 INDICATION
An indication selects a state. It does not name an object, it indicates a side of the distinction.

3 CROSSING
Crossing is the operation that moves from one state to the other. It requires a boundary—a mark—to be crossed.

4 RE-ENTRY
Re-entry occurs when a crossing returns to the state it came from, but from within. It enables recursion and self-reference.

A mark is not an object; it is an operation that produces a state and the possibility of crossing between states.

Distinction is primitive; objects are derivative.
