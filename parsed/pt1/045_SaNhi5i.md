AUTODIDACTIC NATURE
How an unsupervised universe could learn without a teacher.

1. CORE IDEA
A universe can be modeled as a system that updates its effective laws from experience alone - by compressing data, testing regularities, and retaining only what continues to prove useful for shaping the future. No external labels or teacher required.

2. WHY THIS MATTERS
- Explains why these laws why this universe settles on these effective regularities.
- Shifts from static law to law-learning: laws are outcomes, not primitives.
- Makes theory-space navigable: a path can be searched, even without a destination known.

3. THEORY-SPACE
GAUGE FIELDS — STIFF LAWS
SYMMETRIES — CONSTANTS
START — VACUA — DIMENSIONS
A landscape of possible laws. The system wanders, tests, compresses, and retains what works.

4. AUTODIDACTIC PROTOCOLS
- NL LEARNING: Compress experiences across scales; keep what persists under coarse-graining.
- PRECEDENCE: Prefer laws that curve reality more efficiently and codify more data with fewer elements.
- SELF-SAMPLING: Generate internal hypotheses and test them against counter-examples in experience.
- VARIETY: Maintain diverse hypotheses to avoid premature convergence and local traps.
- GEOMETRIC SELF-ASSEMBLY: Discover structures that assemble stably and retain across contexts.

5. CONSEQUENCERS
- MEMORY: Structures store information across time.
- FEEDBACK: Structures influence future experiments.
- SELECTION: What preserves perspective is retained.
Some information becomes historically effective because it is stored in structures that shape the future.
The world remembers what it encodes.

6. WHAT THE SYSTEM LEARNS
- Effective laws (models that compress experience and generalize)
- Stable regularities across scales
- Coarse-grainings that preserve predictive power
- Attractors and basins of stability
- Predictive rules: how states evolve and constrain one another.

7. WHAT IT DOES NOT LEARN AUTOMATICALLY
- Justice
- Compassion
- Forgiveness
- Protection of the vulnerable
- Value of persons
These require normative guidance, not mere pattern detection.

8. KEY DISTINCTION
LEARNING-TO-PREDICT: Descriptive competence. Seeks regularities. Compresses. Predicts. "What will happen?"
LEARNING-TO-VALUE: Normative discernment. Assesses worth. Chooses. Commits. "What should happen?"

9. AUTODIDAXIS IN CONTEXT
RAW EXPERIENCE → COMPRESSION → EFFECTIVE LAWS → PREDICTION & CONTROL
THE BOUNDARY: Autodidaxis explains how order emerges and laws stabilize. It does not, by itself, explain why those laws ought to be just.

AUTODIDAXIS CAN EXPLAIN HOW LAWS STABILIZE. IT DOES NOT YET EXPLAIN WHY A WORLD SHOULD BE JUST.
