CHIRALITY: ORIENTED DISTINCTION

7 / 8

ORIENTATION
Direction gives a distinction an order.

PHASE
Orientation carries a complex phase.

INTERFERENCE
Phases combine. Constructive or destructive.

REACHABILITY
What can be reached depends on how phases align.

ORDINARY ADJACENCY (UNDIRECTED, REAL)
U
V  W
Adjacency is symmetric. No phase. No handedness.

CHIRAL ADJACENCY (DIRECTED, COMPLEX)
U
σ_uv   σ_uw
e^{iθ}
V   σ_vw   W
σ_vu = σ̄_uv

Phase is attached to orientation. Reversing direction conjugates the phase.

σ_uv is unit complex; σ_vu = σ̄_uv

A^σ
The chiral (oriented) adjacency matrix with unit-modulus entries.

A distinction can be more than a cut: it can be oriented. Chirality changes interference and therefore changes what becomes reachable.
