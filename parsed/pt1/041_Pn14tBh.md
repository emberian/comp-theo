JUSTICE FRAME

Restraint is good only when it:

Protects persons from harm  
Prevents wrongdoing  
Preserves the conditions  
of flourishing  
Respects the dignity and  
agency of persons affected  
Is proportionate and revisable

A NECESSARY WARNING

Pure passivity can  
become complicity.

When we could prevent  
wrong and will not,  
we tacitly consent.

GUIDING DISCERNMENT

Know the ends.  
Seek the good  
and avoid evil.

Examine causes.  
Understand what  
your restraint enables  
or prevents.

Consider persons.  
Who is helped?  
Who is harmed?

Choose proportion.  
Least force.  
Most good.

Remain answerable.  
Reassess. Revise.  
Repair.

THESIS

Negation is morally meaningful only when it is  
selective, protective, and answerable to justice.
