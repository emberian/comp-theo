ORDERED CREATION / JUSTICE & LEAST-ACTION SERIES - 7 / 9

WHAT IF NEGACTION IS GOOD?
Restraint, inhibition, and the moral ambiguity of minimizing harmful action.

THREE MODES OF NEGACTION

1. GOOD NEGACTION
Restrains that protects.
- Restraint, nonviolence
- Inhibition of harmful transitions
- Refusal of cruelty
- Interruption of destructive cascade

2. AMBIGUOUS NEGACTION
Unclear motive, uncertain effects.
- Passivity without reflection
- Quietism or withdrawal
- Moral delay
- Bureaucratic nonresponse
- Willful ignorance

3. BAD NEGACTION
Neglect that harms.
- Suppression of flourishing
- Refusal of aid
- Paralysis in the face of suffering
- Neglect that enables injustice

NEGACTION ≠ NIHILISM.
To refrain from certain action, is not to deny value or responsibility.

TRAJECTORIES OF HARM AND THE MORAL POWER OF RESTRAINT
POTENTIAL HARMFUL CAUSES
- Violence
- Cruel Policy
- Deception
- Exploitation
- Ecological Harm

A. BLOCKED: What inhibition prevents harm.
B. DAMPED: Proportionate restraint reduces harm.
C. REDIRECTED: Restraint channels toward good.

GOOD OUTCOMES PRESERVED
- Human Dignity
- Flourishing Communities
- Trust & Justice
- Sustainable Systems
- Knowledge & Truth

U. INDISCRIMINATE SUPPRESSION: All action shut down. Good and bad alike unmet.
Blanket passivity erases good and enables evil.
Stagnation • Suffering • Injustice • Collapse

THE REAL QUESTION
?
Which actions should be prevented, at whose expense?

THE SIGNED GIFT
Harmful action is spared.
Compassionate intervention is preserved.
Not all restraint is wise. Not all good is good. Discernment signs the cost.

JUSTICE FRAME
Restraint is good only when it:
- Protects persons from harm
- Prevents wrongdoing
- Preserves the conditions of flourishing
- Respects the dignity and agency of persons affected
- Is proportionate and revisable

A NECESSARY WARNING
Pure passivity can harm.
When we could prevent wrong and will not, we tacitly consent.

GUIDING DISCERNMENT
- Know the ends: Seek the good and avoid evil
- Examine causes: Understand what your restraint enables or prevents.
- Consider persons: Who is helped? Who is harmed?
- Choose proportion: Least force when possible
- Remain answerable: Restore, account, repair.

THESIS
Negaction is morally meaningful only when it is selective, protective, and answerable to justice.
