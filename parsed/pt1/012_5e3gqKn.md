## Transcription
FROM THE PRIMACY OF DISTINCTION

FROM DISTINCTION TO GRAPH

CENTRAL THESIS
A graph is a stabilized field of distinctions.

THE PROGRESSION

DISTINCTION → REGION → VERTEX → EDGE → WALK

a boundary creates difference

a difference stabilizes as interior

a stable region becomes a vertex (node)

an admissible crossing (relation) connects states

a sequence of admissible crossings forms a walk

G = (V, E)

V = vertex = distinguishable state
E = edge = admissible crossing / relation

Once distinctions are stabilized into states and crossings, a graph of possible traversal appears.

SIDE NOTE
Not every distinction is traversable; adjacency selects lawful passage.
