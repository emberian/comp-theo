THE WOUND RECORDS A DEBT
THE LEDGER OF THE WOUND

Not all wounds bleed. Some leave scars. Both add to the total.

Denial multiplies the debt. Naming begins the return.

WHAT WAS TAKEN
Name it. Do not turn away. The taking began the record.

LOSS
Not only the object, but the order it disturbed.

WITNESS
Someone saw. Someone knows. Nothing is outside memory.

WHAT REMAINS OPEN
The wound does not close until truth is met.

RECORD
Harm becomes inscribed. It can be denied, but not unwritten.

TRUTH
It is not possession. It is alignment with what truly is.

MEMORANDUM
ACCOUNT(S) OPEN
TRUST
BETRAYAL
SILENCE
BROKEN BOND
NEGLECT
HUMILIATION
FAILURE

The ledger does not pretend. It records.

Return is not cancellation. It is reckoning.

A wound is not only pain; it becomes a record
that asks how truth, loss, and return will now be carried.

REMEMBER
Memory is a sacred act. To remember is to refuse false amnesia.

WEIGH
The wound has weight. Justice measures what harm will heal.

WRITE
To write conviction. To witness is to bind to truth.

CARRY
The wound travels with the soul until it is redeemed.

RETURN
Not to what was lost, but to what is made whole.

SEALED IN STRESS
KEPT IN DEBT

computational theology
