## Transcription
CHEAP FUTURES
computational theology

WORLDS MAKE SOME FUTURES CHEAP
AND OTHERS COSTLY.
ARCHITECTURE PRECEDES BEHAVIOR.

WORLD
the total arrangement of places, people, rules, and signals that surrounds the agent.
you do not choose the world. it chooses with you.

ENVIRONMENT
shapes attention, access, and friction.
what is near, repeated, and rewarded becomes the path of least resistance.

REACHABILITY
some goals are close and obvious.
others are distant, unclear, or hidden behind high costs.

INCENTIVE
signals of reward and success pull attention and justify effort.
incentives do not create value; they steer toward it.

ROUTINE
repetition builds grooves of ease.
what is repeated becomes automatic; automatic becomes identity.

POSSIBILITY
the future is not infinite.
what is easy will be tried. what is tried will be lived. what is lived becomes the future.

BLOCKED STEEP NARROW HIGH COST
REMOVE OBSTACLE HARD TO FIND EASY TO IGNORE
LOCAL VISIBLE FREQUENT LOW COST

world-design → path-costs → repeated choices → future
LOWER COSTS GET CHOSEN MORE. WHAT GETS CHOSEN MORE, SHAPES WHAT YOU BECOME.

HOW ENVIRONMENTS TRAIN DESIRE
1. EXPOSURE what is around  2. REPETITION you see it again and again  3. EASE it becomes familiar and low-friction  4. REFERENCE now feels good, you start to want it
shapes desire — costs make / costly — invisible — repeatable

ATTENTION  ACCESS  INFORMATION  SOCIAL PROOF  INCENTIVE  FRICTION  TIME HORIZON  IDENTITY

Practice question: What future does this world make easy to become?
