## Transcription
PATHS HAVE COSTS
computational theology

PEOPLE DO NOT MOVE THROUGH ABSTRACT FREEDOM;
THEY MOVE THROUGH WEIGHTED PATHS.

ALL PATHS ARE WEIGHTED

COST
the total price paid to take a path — time, energy, resources, risk, reputation, and opportunity.
all good paths require something.

FRICTION
resistance that drains momentum and attention.
hidden weights make even good paths hard to walk.

PATH
a sequence of choices connected by edges.
paths are not abstract lines — they are lived routes with real weight.

TRADEOFF
every path gained means something else is given up.
the cost of one good is the forgone good of another.
every gain tilts the scale.

ACCESS
not every path is open to you.
doors require keys, resources, trust, knowledge, or timing.
availability is a weight.

CONSEQUENCE
every path action forward into tomorrow.
costs compound. effects ripple.
nothing ends without leaving a trace.

FREEDOM IS REAL —
BUT THE MAP IS COSTLY.
YOU CHART THE COST,
CHOOSE THE GOOD,
WALK IT ANYWAY.

= choice
= path
= waypoint
= friction

choice = desire + path-cost + friction
pulls you forward — adds weight — slows you down

HOW A PATH BECOMES CHOSEN
1. ATTRACTOR something good calls you  2. PATH you see a route that could lead you there  3. FRICTION weights resist — costs appear  4. WEIGHT repetition lessens friction — it becomes easy  5. DESTINATION you arrive — and become what you walked
notice the attractor — name the path — count the costs — endure the weights — become the end

DISCERNMENT  COURAGE  PATIENCE  WISDOM  CONSTANCY  HOPE

Practice question: Which good path has become too expensive to walk?
