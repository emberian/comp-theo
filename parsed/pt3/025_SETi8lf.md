## Transcription
LAMENT
computational theology

TRUTH
name what hurts
hide nothing from the Holy
truth is the first act of trust

ADDRESS
speak to One who can hold what is unbearable
address creates holy space
you are not shouting into the void

CRY
cry is not weakness
voice gives form to pain
tears are prayer in its purest syntax

RELATION
lament builds relationship
honest pain invites faithful presence
we are known, and still we belong

HE HEARS EVERY CRY. NOT ONE IS LOST.
PSALM 56:8

lament = pain → truth → relation

SIGNAL
grief becomes signal
meaning extracts itself from what wounds
nothing is wasted in the Beloved

THE PATH OF LAMENT

PAIN IS REAL

NAMED IN SPEECH

HEARD IN LOVE

HELD IN MEANING

STILL CONNECTED

do not numb

give it voice

be met with grace

meaning does not erase

love keeps the thread

Blessed are those who mourn, for they shall be comforted. MATTHEW 5:4

ALLOW   HONESTY   COMPASSION   PATIENCE   HOPE   BEAUTY   TRUST

Practice question: Can sorrow be spoken without becoming despair?

## Visual
A dark star-flecked grimoire poster in devotional tone. The center shows two figures: a veiled, grieving robed figure leaning on a stone pedestal and a winged angel pouring a stream of light/tears into a basin between them, set against a deep blue starry night. Symmetrical side panels (Truth, Cry on the left; Address, Relation, Signal on the right) frame the scene with small heart and rose icons. A five-stage horizontal "Path of Lament" diagram with glyphs runs across the lower portion, in indigo, gold, and black illuminated-manuscript etching style.
