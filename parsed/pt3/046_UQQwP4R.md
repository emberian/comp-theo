## Transcription
PROVIDENCE
AS COST-LANDSCAPE ENGINEERING
computational theology

PROVIDENCE IS NOT ONLY COMMAND;
IT IS THE DESIGN OF WORLDS
IN WHICH GOODNESS IS REACHABLE.

PROVIDENCE
the wise care by which the Good shapes the possible so that creatures can truly flourish.

WEIGHTS
every path has a cost: suffering, ignorance, sin, distance, opportunity cost.
weights encode friction and resistance in the world.

MINIMUM PAIN
providence minds minima that connect creatures to good, the shortest path to next becomes the easiest path in practice.

ACCESS
true goodness must be accessible, not distant in principle but near in practice.

DESIGN
the Architect reshapes topology and weights: removing barriers, adding bridges, planting signs, healing paths.

REACHABILITY
goodness is reachable when the minimal cost to a good state is below the threshold of hope and ability.

IGNORANCE — TRUTH — LOVE
SUFFERING — DESPAIR — HEALING — FLOURISHING

providence = world-design → lower-cost good

d(a,b) = min  ⊕ w(e)
p∈Paths(a,b) e∈p

w(e): minimal cost from a to b
w(e): weight (cost) of edge e
min: plus addition (logical sum)
min: choice of minimal-cost path

THE GOAL: MINIMIZE COST TO THE GOOD SO GOOD CAN BE CHOSEN FREELY.

PROVIDENTIAL DESIGN MOVES
1. MAP perceive the world as it is  2. WEIGHT identify stress and barriers  3. TEST remove paths and witness  4. REVISE reweight topology and reduce costs  5. INHABIT enter creatures into the story
