DISCERNMENT
computational theology

DESIRE
a movement of the heart toward what seems good
name it without haste

DISCERN THE SPIRIT OF THE GOOD

CLARITY
truth is seen in stillness and honest attention
illumination precedes right choice

BRING EVERY DESIRE INTO THE LIGHT

THAT YOU MAY CHOOSE WHAT IS GOOD

SACRIFICE
what must die for this to live
count the cost without self-deception

FREEDOM
freedom is power under truth
not escape from it
choose what makes love more free

FRUIT
every desire bears fruit in time
by their fruit you will know them

NOT ALL THAT GLITTERS IS GOOD.
NOT ALL THAT FEELS TRUE IS GOOD.

THE LAMP OF CLARITY.
THE SCALES OF TRUTH.
THE HEART OF COURAGE.

CHOICE
you are not the desire
you can choose what to serve

discernment = desire → examination → fruit

1. FEEL DESIRE
notice the pull

2. INSPECT WORLD

3. NAME GOOD/HARM
what it demands

4. TEST FRUIT?

5. CHOOSE FREELY

## Visual
A gilded grimoire poster in devotional tone. The center shows a winged angel holding a lantern in one hand and a balance scale in the other, above a glowing anatomical heart, set against a luminous landscape with a distant golden city and flaming braziers on stone pillars. Symmetrical side panels (Desire, Sacrifice, Fruit on the left; Clarity, Freedom, Choice on the right) frame the figure. A five-stage horizontal "discernment process" diagram with small icons runs across the lower portion, in warm amber, gold, and black illuminated-manuscript etching style; the lantern, scale, and heart symbolize clarity, truth, and courage.
