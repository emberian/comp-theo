THE WORLD TEACHES BY COST
Anisotropic environments do not merely constrain action; they tutor desire.

1. CORE IDEA
Cost landscape shape what is easy, hard, attractive, and avoided.
High cost / Medium cost / Low cost
Easy / attractive ... Hard / avoided ... Impassable

2. PRIVILEGED CORRIDORS
Low-cost paths accumulate traffic, attention, and value.
- Value concentrates
- Attention follows
- Traffic reinforces
- Stories justify

3. BLOWTORCH WORLD-FORMATION
Intense structure-formation carves channels and voids.
Heat (intensity) / Carves channels / Creates voids / Stabilizes boundaries
Where heat persists, structure solidifies.

4. ACCESSIBLE GOOD
Desire rises when reward is high and traversal cost is low.
High reward & low cost = high desire
High reward & high cost = low desire
Low reward (any cost) = low desire
Low / Medium reward / High
Low ... Traversal Cost ... High

5. EXTERNALITIES
Cheap paths for some may hide costs elsewhere.
Local path appears cheap -> Distant burdens accumulate (elsewhere) -> Benefits enjoyed upstream (virtualized gain)
What is cheap now may be paid for by others later.

6. INSTITUTIONAL COST-SHAPING
Roads, markets, interfaces, and norms engineer preference.
ROADS: Lower friction for movement; Build; Maintain; Prioritize.
MARKETS: Price, signal, and allocate; Subsidize; Tax; Steer.
INTERFACES: Surface options, hide others; Highlight; Rank; Nudge.
NORMS: Define what ought to be; Permit; Prohibit; Shame/Praise.
Institutions don't just inflict worlds; they sculpt cost.

7. INTERIOR CORRELATE
The agent inherits an expectation field from the world.
Expected payoff field / Perceived cost field / Attractor map / Risk & effort channels
Not neutral. Not random. Inherited and shaped.

8. PROVISIONAL THESIS
"What is wanted" is partly a function of what is reachable.
- Reachable options set the menu
- Cost shapes inviting
- Corridors become destiny
- Wanting is tutored by the world
Freedom works within a curriculum it did not write.

SYNTHESIS: THE COST TEACHING LOOP
World (cost structure) -> Agents (adapt & move) -> Traffic (concentrates on cheap corridors) -> Value & Attention (accumulate) -> Institutions (stabilize & reinforce structure) -> Desire (recalibrates to the path)

When a world shapes the path, it also half-authors the wanting.
