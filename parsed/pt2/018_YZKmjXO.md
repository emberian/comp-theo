DISTINCTION ENGINEERING
What a system can mark is what it can predict, value, and suffer.

1. FIRST OPERATION: DRAW A DISTINCTION.
Before "this" or "that," there is only undifferentiated possibility.
To mark a boundary is to open a world of form.
A mark -> not-mark -> form emerges.

2. MARK AND UNMARK: Boundaries create the play of world.
INSIDE / OUTSIDE: Containment and exclusion.
SAME / DIFFERENT: Identity emerges from contrast.
NOISE / FORM: Meaning emerges from what is filtered out.
Unmarking dissolves the boundary; new worlds reappear.

3. DISTINCTIONS SCAFFOLD MODELS AND CATEGORIES.
Models slide on distinction.
Categories are compounded boundaries.
Entities -> Types -> Relations -> System (what to do) (kinds) (among) (world)
Change the distinction -> change the scaffold -> change the model.

4. PREDICTION REQUIRES STABLE DISTINCTIONS; DESIRE INHERITS THEM; PAIN INTENSIFIES AROUND THEM.
PREDICTION: Stability of boundary makes future trackable.
DESIRE: What once is inside our map.
PAIN: Threats to boundaries feel like injury.
Unstable boundaries make futures noisy.
Desire selects out distinctions.
Pain is amplified when distinctions are sharp.
Boundaries shape what we expect, want, and fear.

5. BAD DISTINCTIONS CREATE FALSE GOODS, FALSE THREATS, AND FALSE WOUNDS.
FALSE GOODS: We value what is merely in-group, local, or visible.
FALSE THREATS: We fear what is only different, unfamiliar, or inconvenient.
FALSE WOUNDS: We wound ourselves by miscategorizing, comparison, or illusion.
Mistaken lines mislead the whole system.

6. DISTINCTION REDESIGN: ALTER BOUNDARIES, GRANULARITY, AND FRAMING TO CHANGE COGNITION.
BOUNDARIES: Expand, contract, dissolve, or relocate boundaries.
GRANULARITY: Coarsen or refine the level at which we carve reality.
FRAMING: Shift the axes, the reference, and the purpose.
Redesign the distinction -> redesign the model -> redesign the world.

7. SPENCER-BROWN / LAW-OF-FORMS RESONANCE
The world of form begins with a mark.
No mark (no form): Void.
A mark: Distinction (enables all forms).
To mark is to call something forth. To re-mark is to transform the world.
Keep this conceptual, not biographical.

8. PROVISIONAL THESIS
- ONTOLOGY | What exists is what the distinction allows.
- AGENCY | What can be done is what the distinction makes possible.
- SUFFERING | What can hurt is what the distinction exposes.
Change the way we draw -> change what there is, what we do, and how we can be hurt.

SYNTHESIS: THE DISTINCTION LOOP
DISTINCTION (We mark.) -> PREDICTION (We project.) -> VALUE (We prefer.) -> PAIN (We protect.) -> Feedback refines or corrupts the distinction; The loop is self-reinforcing.

To redraw the distinction is already to redesign the world.
