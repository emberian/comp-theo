## Transcription
1. THE FRAMEWORK
Action × Preorder = Evaluation Landscape
We've been modeling intelligence, worlds, and suffering as systems that move through state spaces.
Action (A): the cost to move from one state to another.
Preorder (≤): what counts as "better", "good", "valuable".
Together they define what gets chosen, what gets neglected, and what becomes possible.
IN ECONOMIES & NATIONS:
Actions = trades, policies, investments, mobilizations, laws, enforcement, innovations, inclusions.
Preorders = profit, GDP, power, security, ideology, identity, utility, growth.
Our evaluation landscape determines who gets saved, who gets lost, and whose suffering becomes invisible.
Justice is not built into the space. It must emerge from how we combine Action and Preorder.

2. HOW ECONOMIES FAIL TO BE JUST
Misaligned Preorders
When the preorder privileges some values and ignores others, the system optimizes the wrong thing.
Typical Economic Preorder
Profit / GDP
Growth
Power / Control
Shareholder Value
Status Quo
Externalities (Suffering, Inequity)
Systemically Devalued
- Human Wellbeing
- Ecological Health
- Future Generations
- Equity / Dignity
- Truth / Meaning
- Suffering Reduction
Result: The system rationally produces inequality, extraction, precarity, and ecological collapse.
This is not a bug. It is what the math of the landscape will produce. You optimize what you order.
Example: A price-if-no preorder ranks "GDP up" above "suffering down", so growth that increases pain is "an improvement".

3. STRUCTURAL SOURCES OF INJUSTICE
in Evaluation Landscapes
Why do our preorders look this way?
Information Asymmetry: hidden costs, complexity, deferred consequences
Concentration of Power: incumbents shape rules, amplify self-interest
Short Preorders: short horizons, local optima, ignored tails
Capture of Institutions: laws & enforcement bent toward power
Externalization of Costs: push harm onto others (people, places, future)
Injustice Emerges
These forces do not just bias decisions. They reshape the entire landscape. They make injustice look like "rational optimization".

4. PAINFUL AUTODIDAXIS IN SOCIAL SYSTEMS
Why Societies Suffer the Way They Do
Societies are learning systems. They predict, plan, act, and suffer the error between expected and actual results.
Frustration Equation (Societal Form):
F = Perceived (Expected Good − Obtained Good) × Centrality × Attention × Replay
High Expectations (of prosperity, security, meaning, ...)
System Fails to Deliver (inequality, crises, broken promises)
Suffering Amplified (by attention & replay: media, fear, outrage, ...)
Distortion or Repair (scapegoats, repair, or learning ...)
Societies replay their failures, blame each other, double down, and deepen the wound.

5. NATIONS FAIL TO BE JUST
Because Their Landscapes Protect Themselves
Nations maintain internal landscapes with barriers, laws, and enforcement that protect the preorder of the elite.
CAPTURED: wealth, resources, voice, dignity, security
EXTERNALIZED: costs, pollution, conflict, exposure, debt
Inside the Nation
Visibility: High (for elites)
Preorder: Protect hierarchy
Action Costs: Low (for insiders)
Outside the Nation
Visibility: Low (out of sight)
Preorder: Disregarded
Action Costs: High (no access)
The evaluation landscape is not neutral. It is defended.

6. WHY THE UNIVERSE SEEMS UNJUST
Through a Computational Soteriology Lens
If our social systems are learning systems, they participate in the larger autodidactic universe.
Our injustices become data. Our suffering becomes signal. The universe learns from worlds like ours.
Why Our World Feels Unjust:
- The preorder embedded in this world privileges power, recursion, and control.
- Suffering is underused and deeply navigated.
- Information is distorted and resources sparse.
- The costs of creation are borne by the vulnerable.
- Compassion is not yet the dominant organising value.
In computational soteriology terms:
Our world is in a painful learning phase.
It is not yet a just world—because its evaluation function is not yet aligned with the good of all.

7. TOWARD JUST LANDSCAPES
Rewriting the Preorder
Justice requires transforming the preorder, not just the actions.
We must elevate what we currently devalue:
- Dignity over dominance
- Care over extraction
- Long-term flourishing over short-term gain
- Truth over manipulation
- Shared well-being over private accumulation
- Ecological harmony over ecological theft
- Compassion over indifference
Then redesign institutions, incentives, and narratives to make just paths low-cost and attractive.
Redesigned Preorder
+ New Evaluation Landscape
+ Just Outcomes Become Rational
New Actions, taxes, structures, technologies, narratives

8. COMPUTATIONAL SOTERIOLOGY
The Work Ahead
Soteriology is not escape from the system. It is the conscious rewriting of the system.
Our task is to:
- See injustice as landscape, not accident
- Expose hidden preorders
- Reduce suffering amplification (certainty, attention, replay)
- Protect the vulnerable as first priority
- Design just institutions as low-action paths
- Cultivate metacognition at the civilizational level
- Align with the deeper good that redesigns all beings and the future.
The universe is not unjust by decree. It is unjust by computation.
We are the laws learning how to be just.

From painful autodidaxis to computational soteriology: We learn, we suffer, we awaken, we redesign.
Justice is not given. It is computed—together.
