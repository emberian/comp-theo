SELF AS LONG-TERM PERFORMANCE EVALUATION
Error becomes personal when the system evaluates itself across time.

1. WHAT IS A SELF?
A self is not a metaphysical substance. It is a persistent object of evaluation and control—a model that represents "me" across time, against which performance is judged and action is guided.

2. THE SELF-MODEL
A system represents its own body, capacities, goals, and limits.
- Body/state: what I am.
- Capacities: what I can do.
- Goals/values: what matters.
- Limits/risks: what can go wrong.

3. LONGITUDINAL SCOREKEEPING
The self keeps score across time: past performance, current state, future prospects.
PAST (What I did) -> PRESENT (Where I am) -> FUTURE (What matters)

SCORECARD
Goal Achievement ... 78%
Skill Growth ... 63%
Reliability ... 81%
Social Standing ... 56%
Health / Energy ... 67%
Integrity ... 84%
OVERALL ... 72%

4. WHY SELF INTENSIFIES PAIN
Error / Failure (outcome < target)
Compared to Self-Model (discrepancy detected)
Self-Relevant Meaning (about me across time)
PAIN

5. SELF-PRESERVATION
Survival and continuity make error signals weightier.
- Threat to goals.
- Threat to future.
- Threat to identity.
- Loss is amplified.
CONTINUITY / PERSIST

6. SHAME, GUILT, INADEQUACY
Socially or self-mediated forms of performance evaluation.
SHAME: Evaluated by others. I feel exposed or deficient in their eyes.
GUILT: Evaluated by my own values. I feel I have done what I ought to do.
INADEQUACY: Evaluated by comparison. I feel I don't measure up.
All are self-referential evaluations that amplify error into suffering.

7. BEYOND EGO
Distributed cognition may still generate self-like coordination without a single homunculus.
COORDINATION HUB (self-model)
The self is a pattern of integration, not a point of location.

CORE RELATION
suffering ~ error signal x self-relevance
felt pain ; discrepancy from goal, value, or norm ; how much it matters to this self across time

A self is what turns local failure into a history about 'me'.
