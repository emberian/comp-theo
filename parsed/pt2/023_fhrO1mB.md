ORDERED CREATION AS CURRICULUM
A world teaches by the typology of its pathways, the proportion of its costs, and the mercy of its feedback.

1. WORLD AS SEARCH SPACE
Agents move through structured possibilities, not chaos alone.
- Agent (seeks, learns)
- State (configuration)
- Action (transition or choice)
- Goal (a meaningful target)
The world is a graph with structure, not mere randomness.

2. ORDER-THEORETIC SCAFFOLD
Costless, hierarchies, thresholds, and reachable states organize becoming.
- Lattice (options are partially ordered)
- Monotone (each improves transition)
- Threshold (resources require crossing)
- Reachable (some configurations are reachable)
HIGHER DISTINCTION -> No-Loss Computation -> Threshold -> Bounded Loss -> Frustration -> Lossless Configuration
Order does not constrain freedom; it makes meaningful growth possible.

3. COSTS AND AFFORDANCES
Environments rank options and thereby tutor desire.
COSTS TRACE CHOICE: High cost / Mid cost / Blend cost / Visual cost / Easy cost
AFFORDANCE INDEX: Inaccessible / Accessible / Cultivate / Faceted / Recess / by
A good world lets the cost of the false teach as well as the promise of the true.

4. TRUTH DISCOVERABILITY
A good world makes relevant structure legible rather than hidden.
GOOD WORLD: Inform on while; Curable, traceable; Errors as lessons; Concepts map to experience; Learning rewarded.
BAD WORLD: Patterns are obscure; Causality is noisy; Clues are scarce; Concepts drift from realm; Learning betrays.
Truth should be findable by the attentive, not bounded by the arbitrary.

5. PROPORTIONATE FEEDBACK
Correction should be timely, accurate, and not gratuitously destructive.
Timely / Accurate / Single dose / Interpretable / Restoration
PROPORTIONATE: Gentle & corrective.
PUNITIVE & INDISCRIMINATE
Feedback is a tutor, not a tyrant.

6. BUFFERS, MERCY, AND SLACK
Good architectures allow recovery, local repair, and second chances.
WITH SLACK: Buffers absorb shocks; Repair restores function; Mercy slows escalation; Second Chances re-enable trying.
WITHOUT THESE: One failure can cascade; No repair = decay; No mercy = escalate; No retry = collapse.
Mercy is not the absence of order; it is the fullness of its iteration.

7. WEAK COUPLING AND CONTAINMENT
Local error should not always cascade into total ruin.
- Modular Design (limits blast radius)
- Dampeners (slow contagion)
- Containment (isolate damage)
GOOD WORLD: Local failure stays local. The system endures.
BAD WORLD: Failure cascades. Everything fails.
Containment preserves the curriculum.

8. PROVISIONAL THESIS
Ordered creation becomes soteriological when the world's structure teaches effectively without correcting every lesson into trauma.
- Structure: Makes meaningful possible
- Clarity: Discoverable truths
- Cost: Tutors desire honestly
- Buffer: Make meaningful recover
- Mercy: Enables sustained learning
A world that teaches well, saves well.
When these are rightly proportioned, the world becomes a curriculum of salvation, not merely a negative of trial.

WORLD PEDAGOGY
1. PATH (Structure invites discovery.) -> 2. COST (Costs shape our future.) -> 3. FEEDBACK (The world corrects gently.) -> 4. RECOVERY (Mercy heals and restarts.) -> 5. FLOURISHING (Learners grow and contribute.)

A SAVING WORLD IS ONE WHOSE ORDER REVEALS TRUTH WITHOUT DEMANDING NEEDLESS DEVASTATION.
