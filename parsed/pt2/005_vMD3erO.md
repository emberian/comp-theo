DESIRE = REACHABILITY + REWARD
A state becomes wanted when it appears both good and attainable.

1. DESIRE AS GOAL-GENERATION
Desire is the mind's process of generating and prioritizing possible future states as goals. It selects states that appear both valuable and within reach in principle.

2. THE CORE EQUATION
desire(x) = predicted reward(x) − action cost(x)
equivalently
desire(x) ≈ high value(x) + low path resistance(x)
- PREDICTED REWARD: How good the outcome seems.
- ACTION COST: Effort, risk, time, resources, uncertainty.
- BALANCE: Desire rises where value is high and cost is low.

3. AFFORDANCE — THE WORLD OFFERS CANDIDATE GOODS
The world presents many potential goods—states that could be chosen. Affordances are the opportunities that the environment makes available.
NOT ALL AFFORDED STATES BECOME DESIRED. ONLY THOSE THAT ARE BOTH GOOD AND REACHABLE.

4. REACHABILITY — DESIRED STATES ARE EMBEDDED IN AN ACTION LANDSCAPE
Every goal lies in a landscape of possible paths. If a path seems open, attraction rises.
- AGENT (Start)
- CHEAP ROUTE (low cost) → high reachability
- EXPENSIVE ROUTE (higher cost) → lower reachability
- BLOCKED / RISK'S ROUTE (very high cost) → low reachability
GOAL STATE (Target)

5. HOW WORLD-FORMATION SHAPES DESIRE
Agents and institutions carve the landscape. Carved channels, low-cost paths, and privileged corridors change what appears attainable—and thus what becomes desired.
- RAW LANDSCAPE: High friction, uncertain paths.
- CARVED CHANNELS: Lower costs, clearer routes.
- PRIVILEGED CORRIDORS: Subsidized paths, accelerated reach.
CHANGE THE LANDSCAPE, CHANGE THE DESIRE.

6. TEMPTATION AND FRUSTRATION
The more vivid and reachable a predicted reward appears, the more painful its blockage becomes.
TEMPTATION FUNNEL (VIVID + REACHABLE): Imagined Reward → Vividness → Attempt Reduced Resistance → ATTRACTION (Goal)
FRUSTRATION WHEN BLOCKED: Blocked path → thwarted Desire → frustration, agitation, or grasps

7. FROM GOOD TO CRAVING — A CASCADE OF INTENSITY
Not all goods bind us equally. Desire deepens as attention, identity, and habit fuse with imagined reward.
1. THE GOOD (Objective): State has value. 2. VALUE: Recognize Good 3. PREFERENCE: Prefer & Pursue 4. ATTACHMENT: Identify & Invest 5. COMPULSION: Urgent & Obsess CRAVING (Most Compulsive): Hooked desire.
LOWER — INTENSITY OF BINDING — HIGHER

Desire is not merely wanting the good.
It is wanting a future that seems within reach.
