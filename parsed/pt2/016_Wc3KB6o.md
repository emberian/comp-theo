SOCIAL REPLAY MACHINES
Cultures remember, amplify, and transmit what isolated minds would forget.

1. CORE CLAIM
Groups replay events through story, ritual, gossip, record, and platform.
event (what is told) -> narration (what is told) -> circulation (who sees it, shares it) -> replay (what it makes salient) -> identity (what we become)

2. COLLECTIVE SALIENCE
What many attend to becomes socially real.
ATTENTION CONVERGENCE / SOCIAL REALITY
social reality <- attention -> repetition -> legitimacy (replayed) (validated)

3. SHAME, PRAISE, SCANDAL, AND GRIEVANCE AS PUBLIC REPLAY SIGNALS
SHAME: We expose.
PRAISE: We elevate.
SCANDAL: We circulate.
GRIEVANCE: We rehearse.
MORAL EMOTION + AUDIENCE + NARRATIVE = AMPLIFIED REPLAY

4. INSTITUTIONS AS MEMORY DEVICES
Archives, schools, feeds, and ceremonies.
ARCHIVES (Store and index the past) / SCHOOLS (Teach interpretation) / FEEDS (Stream and rank salience) / CEREMONIES (Embody and rehearse meaning)
Institutions decide what is remembered, how it is told, and how often.

5. TRAUMA TRANSMISSION: REPLAYED PUBLIC RESHARING CAN BIND GENERATIONS
GENERATION 1: Real-world emotion and recall.
GENERATION 2 (Stories transmit): Linked to identity and "never again".
GENERATION 3: Attached to symbols, places, and dates.
Public reshapings shape the chain.
Result: protective wisdom or inherited hypervigilance.

6. NORM FORMATION: REPLAY SELECTS WHAT COUNTS AS ADMIRABLE, DANGEROUS, OR TABOO
REPLAY FILTERS: Frequency, Emotional charge, Source authority, Narrative fit.
NORM OUTCOMES: Admirable, Dangerous, Authority signal, Taboo.
CULTURAL EFFECT: Cohesion, Belonging, Memory, Solidarity.

7. INTERVENTION POINTS: CHANGE THE REPLAY
- DIVERSIFY MEMORY | Include neglected stories and perspectives.
- REPLAY COMPULSIVE AMPLIFICATION | Slow replays that inflame without insight.
- IMPROVE FRAMING | Add context, causes, and complexity.
- CREATE WISER REVIEW | Build practices of deliberation and repair.
- RITUALIZE REVITALIZATION | Turn cycles of blame into cycles of learning.

8. PROVISIONAL THESIS
Societies have cognition like replay dynamics.
- They preserve through attention.
- They remember through repetition.
- They learn through transmission.
- They decide through review.
- They become through identity.
Change the replay, change the society.

THE SOCIAL REPLAY LOOP
EVENT (what happens) -> NARRATION (how it is told) -> CIRCULATION (who sees and shares) -> REPLAY (what it teaches) -> IDENTITY (who we become)
Feedback: Replay reinforces stories, attention, and narration.

A culture suffers where it repeatedly teaches itself to remember only the wound.
