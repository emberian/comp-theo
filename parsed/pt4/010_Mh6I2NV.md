The Forgiveness Suite

FORGIVENESS AS COST REASSIGNMENT

MERCY

SOMEONE ABSORBS THE COST.
You acknowledge
the wound and
take responsibility
for your response.

THE CHAIN CAN STOP HERE.
You have the
power to end
the cycle,
not extend it.

DO NOT PASS THE WOUND FORWARD.
Refuse to hand
your pain to the
next in line.

TO CARRY IS NOT TO COPY.
Strength is not
measured by what
you return,
but what you
refuse to repeat.

I RECEIVE THE COST

I REFUSE TO REPLICATE THE HARM

TRANSFORMATION INSTEAD OF REPLICATION.
What was meant
to poison is
transmuted into
wisdom and mercy.

YOU BECOME A DIFFERENT KIND OF LINK.
One that brings
light, not more
weight, to the
chain.

I CHOOSE TRANSFORMATION OVER REVENGE.

Forgiveness does not pretend the wound was free;
it refuses to make others pay for it again.

ACKNOWLEDGE
Name the harm
without denial.
Truth is the
first act of
responsibility.

ABSORB
You take the
cost so that
others do not
have to carry
what is not
theirs.

TRANSFORM
What could
become poison
becomes
purpose in
your hands.

RELEASE
Let it end
with you.
Do not pass
the wound
forward.

REPAIR
Use what
you've
learned to
build, not
to break.

MERCY INTERRUPTS

THE CYCLE OF HARM

computational theology
