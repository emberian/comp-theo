TWO KINDS OF SCARS. ONE STRUCTURAL TRUTH.

HUMAN TRAUMA | BOTH | MACHINE TRAINING SCARS

Overwhelming events and chronic stress. | Overload distorts learning. | Biased, low-quality, or non-representative data.
Body signals danger too early or too late. | Signals get misweighted. | Features overweighted, underweighted, or ignored.
Avoidance protects in the short term. | Avoidance narrows exploration. | The model avoids regions it cannot predict well.
Memory fragments and generalizes. | Generalization becomes distorted. | Spurious correlations become mistaken for truth.
Recovery needs safety, time, and new experiences. | Repair needs new input and new structure. | Repair needs better data, architecture, and evaluation.

[Left margin, top to bottom:]
THE BODY REMEMBERS BY CHANGING THE COST OF THE PATH.

Pain leaves residue.

Certain routes become costly, risky, or blocked.

Attention narrows.

The future space is reorganized.

Some doors become expensive.

SCARS ARE NOT ONLY MEMORIES.
+
They are edits to the topology of possibility.

HEALING IS TO RE-OPEN POSSIBILITIES.
+
Safety, attunement, and time allow new paths to be tried, trusted, and integrated.

We do not choose all that happened.
+
But we can shape what it makes more or less accessible.

[Right margin, top to bottom:]
MODELS ALSO INHERIT BRITTLENESS, AVOIDANCE, AND SKEWED SALIENCE.

Training leaves imprints.

Some correlations are overlearned or censored.

The model avoids, overreacts, or collapses.

What seems likely is reweighted.

The space of plausible outputs is reshaped.

NOT MALICE— BUT MECHANISM.
+
The model is not cruel. It is trained. It does what the signals reward.

RETRAINING IS TO RE-WRITE POSSIBILITIES.
+
Better data. Clearer goals. Safer feedback. More coverage. New futures.

MODELS do not choose their data.
+
But we choose how we train what we deploy and where we trust it.
