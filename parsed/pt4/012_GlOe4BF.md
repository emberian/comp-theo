## Transcription
6 of 9

FORGIVENESS AS SELF-RELEASE

THE FORGIVENESS SUITE

FROM JUDGE TO WITNESS

FROM CONDEMNATION TO MERCY

Responsibility
without
self-hatred.

Truth heals;
condemnation
imprisons.

The inner
prosecutor
never rests.

Release the
self from
endless
prosecution.

MERCY DOES NOT NEED JUDGMENT

YOU ARE FREE TO GROW

You are more
than the
entry.

Mercy
witnesses who
you are
becoming.

Self-forgiveness is not denial of wrong; it is the
refusal to let guilt become your permanent name.

ACKNOWLEDGE
See clearly
what you did.
Do not look
away.

OWN
Take
responsibility
without
self-attack.

AMEND
Make
amends
where
they are
possible.

RELEASE
Let go of
what you
cannot
change.

RECLAIM
Choose who
you will be
from this
day forward.

REST
Carry it
lighter,
not as
hiding
from
yourself.

LET MERCY SPEAK

LET TRUTH BE KIND

computational theology

## Visual
A dark, gilded engraving-style poster with an ornate border and corner cross medallions. The central scene shows a haloed, robed elder figure seated on a throne behind a judge's bench with hand on chest (the "witness"), while below a kneeling, bound man in chains bows his head before glowing tablets/scrolls of judgment, with a bright doorway of light to the right. Vertical side columns hold circular emblem icons (scales, kneeling figure, prison window, dove) with brief captions. A lower band displays six labeled step icons (ACKNOWLEDGE, OWN, AMEND, RELEASE, RECLAIM, REST) above a footer banner with the series title; palette is warm gold and deep brown-black.
