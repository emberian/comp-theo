## Transcription
THE LEDGER OF THE WOUND

FORGIVENESS AS DE-DEMONIZATION

5 of 9

TRUTH WITHOUT TOTALIZATION.
Name the act
clearly, but
refuse to make
it the whole
person.

DO NOT ENTHRONE THE DEMON-IMAGE.
Imagination can
make a monster
larger than
reality.

THE PERSON IS NOT THE WHOLE INJURY.
The wound
is real, but
the soul is not
exhausted by it.

RELEASE CARICATURE, KEEP CLARITY.
Let go of the
distortion.
Hold fast to
discernment.

ONE ACT DOES NOT DEFINE THE WHOLE.

BOUNDARIES ARE NOT BARRIERS TO MERCY; THEY ARE CONTAINERS OF TRUTH.

BOUNDARY REMAINS.
Forgiveness
does not cancel
protection.
It preserves it.

SEE THE WHOLE.
People are more
than their worst
act—yet never
excused by it.
Held in truth,
opened to
transformation.

Forgiveness names the harm
without turning the other into
nothing but the harm.

DO NOT CONFUSE FORGIVENESS WITH TRUST.
Trust is rebuilt.
Mercy is
freely given.

JUSTICE SEEKING REPAIR.
Forgiveness
walks with
justice, not
around it.

THE SOUL REMAINS FREE.
Refuse to be
imprisoned by
hatred or
fantasy.

LEAVE ROOM FOR CHANGE.
The boundary
keeps the
door from
slamming so
change can
come.

MERCY IS NOT AMNESIA.
It is memory
without
vengeance.

SEALED IN TRUTH

KEPT IN MERCY

computational theology

## Visual
A dark, gilded engraving-style poster with an ornate border and corner scale/coin medallions. The central image splits a single profile face down the middle: the left half is a monstrous, horned, demonic visage with embers, dissolving into a swirl of particles that reform on the right half into a calm, sorrowful human man with a hand on his chest, separated by a luminous golden vertical arc. Vertical side columns hold circular emblem icons (scales, portrait, shield, cage, eye) with captioned aphorisms. A lower band shows five labeled icons (DO NOT CONFUSE, JUSTICE SEEKING, THE SOUL REMAINS, LEAVE ROOM, MERCY IS NOT) above a footer banner with the series title.
