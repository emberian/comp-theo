## Transcription
QVOD SERVAT, ID LIBERAT
XVII
17

SACRED
PROTOCOLS
DESIGNED INTERRUPTIONS THAT PROTECT WHAT IS TRUE AND GOOD.

THE PILLARS

PRAYER
Aligns will. Sets wisdom. Bends toward what is good.

METTA
Goodwill in thought, word, and deed. Extends care.

SABBATH
A holy rest. A weekly refusal to be devoured.

SLOW THINKING
Think deeply. Imagine consequence. Resist haste.

CONTEMPLATIVE PAUSE
Space between stimulus and response. A sacred breath.

DESIGNED INTERRUPTIONS
Break autopilot. Invite reflection. Reclaim agency.

PRACTICE IS MERCY PREPARED IN ADVANCE.

THE VOW
We design for human dignity, compassion, and truth. We submit power to constraints. We protect the vulnerable.

CAPTURE IS SILENT. PROTOCOL IS LOUDER. CHOOSE BEFORE PRESSURE.

1 PAUSE
Make space. Slow down. Do not react.

2 NOTICE
What is happening? What do I notice?

3 DISCERN
What is true? What is helpful? What is aligned?

4 CHECK
May this action reduce suffering and increase well-being.

5 RETURN
Act with integrity. Return to the sacred aim.

6 REST
Release. Restore. Trust the rhythm.

GOOD PROTOCOLS ARE MERCIFUL CONSTRAINTS THAT KEEP SYSTEMS FROM COLLAPSING INTO THEIR CHEAPEST LOOPS

Run the protocols. Verify. Look again.

CONTEMPLATIVE PAUSE IS A DESIGN CHOICE. IT BREAKS CAPTURE LOOPS. IT PROTECTS THE FUTURE.

PERSON
- Daily prayer and intention
- Practice metta and compassion
- Keep a weekly sabbath (no extraction)
- Journal and reflect before acting
- Use pauses before sending or posting
- Seek counsel and correction
- Confess, repair, and return

COMMUNITY
- Open with silence or shared intention
- Speak to uplift, not to dominate
- Keep sabbath as a community
- Decide with consent and clarity
- Build in review and ascent clauses
- Protocol to vulnerable by design
- Practice generosity, gratitude, and repair

MACHINE
- Human oversight at key decisions
- Red-team before release
- Staged deliberation (draft, challenge, refine)
- Tool and data checks (inputs, provenance, drift)
- Handle uncertainty with humility
- Honor review before consequential action
- Audit, log, and continuous improvement

EVALUATION PROTOCOLS
- Define success beyond metrics
- Test for harm, bias, and misuse
- Stress test and red-team
- Monitor and feedback
- Publish and be accountable
- Sunset or renegotiate

KEEP THE AIM

SERVE THE REAL

A HOLY RULE IS NOT A CAGE.
IT IS A PATH OF RETURN.
