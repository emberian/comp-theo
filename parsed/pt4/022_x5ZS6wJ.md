GRACE AND REPENTANCE

III OF IX

THEME: GRACE DOES NOT REMOVE THE NEED TO TURN;
IT MAKES TRUTHFUL TURNING POSSIBLE WITHOUT ANNIHILATION.

GRACE DOES NOT CRUSH THE TRUTHS NEED, NOR QUENCH THE SMOLDERING WICK

TRUTH WITHOUT ANNIHILATION
Truth reveals
what is real
without destroying
what is valuable.

MERCY ENABLES HONESTY
Love makes
it safe to stop
lying—to others
and to yourself.

TURNING MADE POSSIBLE
Grace removes
the paralysis
of shame and
makes turning
possible.

TURN FROM WHAT IS FALSE
Repentance is
not asked. It
is alignment
with what is
true.

SAFE ENOUGH TO REPENT
You can face
what is wrong
because you are
held in what
is right.

NO CONDEMNATION IN THE TURN
There is no
condemnation
for those who
are in Christ.
— ROMANS 8:1

SHAME DECEIVES YOU

TRUTH CALLS YOU

YOU ARE SEEN FULLY,
YOU ARE NOT DISCARDED.
YOU ARE INVITED TO TURN.

rehabilitated.

WALK IN TRUTH, LIVE IN GRACE
The path
continues.
Your feet
are not
alone.

TURNING MADE POSSIBLE

RECOGNIZE
See clearly
what is.

CONFESS
Agree with truth
without evasion.

TURN
Choose to turn
toward the light.

RECEIVE
Accept mercy
in deeper joy.

RENEW

with what is true.

RETURN

in freedom.

O God, who does not despise a sincere heart,
make us honest without fear, and turn us without delay;
that in Your truth we may find our freedom,
and in Your mercy, our home.

## Visual
A dark, gilded engraving-style poster with an ornate border and corner Roman-numeral/cross medallions. The central image shows a robed figure walking a glowing path toward a radiant heart-shaped light suspended in a brooding sky over ruined arches, with an inset oval scene below of a kneeling figure receiving light. Vertical side columns hold circular emblem icons (book, doorway, scales, leaf, tree) with captioned aphorisms about truth and mercy; four label tablets repeat key phrases. A lower band displays six labeled step icons (RECOGNIZE, CONFESS, TURN, RECEIVE, RENEW, RETURN) above a prayer banner and footer maxims, in warm gold and deep brown-black tones.
